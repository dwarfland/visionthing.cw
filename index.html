Spanish Verbs for Mac

Learning Spanish does not have to be harder than it needs to be. Verbs lets you keep track of the verbs as you learn them and browse the different conjugations.

Add more verbs as you go along, and have Verbs conjugate regular verbs for you automatically. Manually adjust for irregularities. Show and hide the conjugations you need at the time. Export your verbs to .html.

Get it on the App Store.

Trails for iPhone

Record and revisit the trails you walk, hike, run or cycle.

Trails is a simple and easy-to-use app that lets you record trails as you explore them. Share your trails with others, or revisit your favorites in the future. Learn how far you walked, how high you climbed, and more.

Get it on the App Store.

Curaçao Weather for iOS

The official Weather App for the island and its surroundings.

Curaçao Weather provides you with up-to-date weather information and forecast, a map view with rain and cloud overlay, as well as general information about the island and its beautiful 80+ beaches and snorkel/dive sites.

Read more on curaçaoweatherapp.com.

Get it on the App Store, 100% free.

Read the Case Study on how Curaçao Weather was developed, using the Elements and Oxygene programming language from RemObjects Software.

Privacy Policy

Our apps are committed to preserving your privacy.

Spanish Verbs for Mac

The app does not collect any data without your permission and never communicates with our servers or third parties across the network.

Curaçao Weather App

The app does not collect any data without your permission and, in normal operation, does not transmit any data about you to our servers.

If and only if you choose to submit impacts via the dedicated “Report an Impact” feature, the data you explicitly provide for the report will be transmitted to our servers for further processing by the app and/or the Meteorological Department. Data collected as part of a report includes the optional photo and description or comment you provide, and may also include your current GPS location, if you choose to enable that option.

The app uses Sign in with Apple and Google Sign-in for submitting reports, to avoid spam and abuse. The reports you submit will be internally linked to your account in case the Weather Department needs to follow up with you, but no information about your account will be made available to anybody. Only the type of impact and the rough location will become visible to other users of the app.
